This is a special recipe for me because the first time I ever made it was for our second baby’s [gender reveal ](https://www.thisismymomoir.com/blog/planning-gender-reveal)party!  I wanted everything to be perfect and had an idea in my mind of what I wanted to make, but I couldn’t find exactly what I was looking for online.  I ended up using a combination of two recipes from websites I love, [Cafe Delites](https://cafedelites.com/) and[ Delish](https://www.delish.com/).  Our whole family loved this casserole and I have continued to make this version that I created whenever we host a brunch at our house.  

I love this recipe because you can really make it your own with whatever fillings you like (sometimes I stick to a veggie version or just cheese, or I will substitute ham for the bacon).  The hash browns on the bottom add a nice crunch and make it a more filling meal.  A lot of breakfast bakes have a bread base, so I like that this is a little bit lighter, since I always serve it with fruit and something sweet like [homemade muffins](https://www.thisismymomoir.com/blog/healthy-baking).  I hope you enjoy it!



**Breakfast Casserole with Hash Browns Recipe**

Makes 8 servings

**Ingredients**

- 1 pound bacon, chopped

- 10 large eggs

- 3/4 cup 2% milk 

- 1 teaspoon garlic powder

- Salt to season

- Fresh cracked black pepper

- 1 cup shredded white Cheddar

- 28 oz shredded hash browns, frozen

- 1/2 cup shredded mozzarella cheese

- Fresh chives



**Instructions**

- Preheat oven to 375 

- Lightly grease an 8x12-inch baking dish with nonstick oil spray (I love my [Staub casserole dish](https://amzn.to/3ZnU06n))

- Add bacon to pan and cook until crispy

- Remove bacon but reserve 2 tablespoons of bacon fat in pan

- Blot excess grease from bacon, chop into small pieces

- Sautee hash browns in bacon fat until defrosted and golden

- In a large bowl, whisk together eggs, milk and garlic powder

- Season generously with salt and pepper 

- Mix in the bacon and cheddar cheese

- Add a layer of hash browns to the base of the dish

- Pour egg mixture over the hash browns 

- Top with the mozzarella 

- Bake until eggs are cooked through and edges start to turn golden brown (about 35-40 minutes)
