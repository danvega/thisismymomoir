I absolutely love to bake.  It makes me so happy to get out all of my ingredients and just zone out and create something from scratch.  I also love to cook, but many times that feels more like more of a means to an end (must feed family) than a creative outlet.  Baking is a much more soulful experience for me.  There’s something magical about having freshly baked chocolate chip cookies prepared for my kids when they come in cold from playing in the snow.  It’s even more enjoyable when I know that what I have baked for my family is from wholesome, less processed ingredients.

As much as I love to bake, I also like to fit into my clothes, so discovering healthy swaps for baking was a really fun learning process for me.  This all began when my husband was diagnosed with Type 2 diabetes.  At first, I was so worried for him and we decided to be really strict with our diet.  But once he started getting his blood sugar under control, we learned that a treat here and there wasn’t going to derail all of his progress, and a healthier version of a treat is much better than a piece of birthday cake (although we absolutely still enjoy that on special occasions as well).  I don’t create my own baking recipes, so I searched and sampled recipes from various websites and ended up coming back to the same ones for their wholesome ingredients and delicious outcomes.  

My absolute favorite site for baking is [Cookie & Kate](https://cookieandkate.com/).  This is where I really learned how to make healthy swaps that still taste amazing.  These baking recipes have become staples in my house, to the point that I can make a lot of her recipes from memory.  Her cookie recipe listed below is “my” specialty cookie that my family always requests, and the muffin recipes are on rotation fairly regularly as well.  

A note on the muffins, 1) use an ice cream scooper to portion, they come out the perfect size! 2) They taste better the next day!  The flavor develops overnight in the refrigerator and I actually like to eat them cold (my husband heats them up in the microwave for about 20 seconds) but they taste much sweeter and more flavorful the next day. Her site also led me to attempt baking my own donuts, and they turned out great!  Who knew buying some [donut pans](https://amzn.to/3kxJquF) would be the start of such a fun adventure?  My grandma actually worked in a bakery making donuts when I was little and learning to make these healthier versions makes me feel close to her (and I think she would be proud of me for making them a little bit healthier!)

My kids love donuts, so knowing how to make a healthier version that still tastes amazing but are so much lower in fat and refined sugars makes me happy.  Katie’s recipes offer spelt flour as an option for donuts and that is what I choose when I make them.  I read in **Kristin Cavallari’s cookbook, “**[True](https://amzn.to/3mf7Jyc)[ ](https://amzn.to/3mf7Jyc)[Roots](https://amzn.to/3mf7Jyc)**”** that her (now ex) husband also has diabetes and that spelt flour is better for managing blood sugar, so I loved that this was an option for these recipes.
