This is a lightened-up version of my [Green Smoothie](https://www.thisismymomoir.com/blog/green-smoothie) recipe for days when I am less active and don’t need as many calories. It’s still a great source of protein and healthy fats and tastes delicious. This is a recipe my kids actually love too! It’s a great way to sneak added nutrition into their diets. I doubt they would love it as much if they knew they were drinking spinach ;)  I make a few substitutions in the kid's version of the smoothie, which I will share below.  My girls love drinking their smoothies out of these cute and easy-to-wash [containers](https://amzn.to/3kxJPNK).

I love alternating this smoothie and the green smoothie because seeds are such a great source of nutrition that a lot of people are missing in their diets. This way you are alternating two amazing superfoods throughout your week. Flax and chia seeds are also great in [oatmeal](https://www.thisismymomoir.com/blog/strawberry-chia-oatmeal), which we also eat a few times a week (sometimes I even have [oatmeal](https://www.thisismymomoir.com/blog/pb-banana-oatmeal) for lunch!) I hope your family enjoys this healthy smoothie recipe as much as our family does. Enjoy!

**Strawberry Chia Smoothie recipe**

2 large handfuls spinach

5-6 frozen strawberries

1 container of Greek yogurt (I love Oikos Triple Zero strawberry because it’s high in protein and low in sugar)

2T chia seeds

1 cup original vanilla almond milk

Add all ingredients to [blender](https://amzn.to/3Y2i2Tf) and blend until smooth 



**Strawberry Chia Smoothie- for kids!**

Makes 1 large or 2 small servings (My young kids split this)

1 large handful spinach

5-6 frozen strawberries

1 small or 1/2 large banana (for extra carbs and extra flavor)

1 container yogurt (My kids love Stonyfield brand strawberry or strawberrry banana)

1 T chia seeds

1 cup almond milk (or regular 2% dairy milk)

Add all ingredients to blender and blend until smooth
